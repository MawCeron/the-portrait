## Character Arcs

### Francesco del Giocondo:

- **Start:** Ambitious merchant who wants cultural recognition to transcend merchant status
- **Middle:** Becomes obsessed with Leonardo, the chase becomes his identity, experiences daughter's death (1518)
- **Crisis:** Arrives in France the day Leonardo dies (May 2, 1519), sees portrait but walks away
- **Resolution:** Leonardo's letter reveals their friendship was real, Francesco finds peace
- **End:** Lives 19 more years telling Leonardo stories, dies content

### Lisa Gherardini:

- **Start:** Practical 24-year-old merchant's wife, bemused by husband's ambitions
- **Middle:** Endures erosion of marriage, financial strain, Francesco's absences
- **Realization:** Understands the chase is what Francesco needs (Chapter 8)
- **Acceptance:** Stops fighting it, becomes witness rather than opponent
- **Wisdom:** Guides Francesco to peace after reading Leonardo's letter
- **End:** Reflects on how the portrait captured more than her face

### Leonardo da Vinci:

- **Character through others' eyes:** Brilliant, distracted, kind, impossible
- **Function:** Mirror for Francesco's ambition and longing
- **Reveal:** The letter shows he valued Francesco's friendship, felt less alone
- **Legacy:** Not just the painting but the connection it represented

## Key Relationships

### Francesco & Leonardo:

- Unequal friendship that was nonetheless real
- Leonardo needed Francesco's persistence and normal-person perspective
- Francesco needed Leonardo to feel connected to something greater
- Never fully articulated while both alive
- Letter provides validation Francesco desperately needed

### Francesco & Lisa:

- Long marriage, deep love despite erosion
- Lisa as witness, not opponent (after Chapter 8)
- She understands him better than he understands himself
- Physical intimacy fades during the chase years
- She guides him to peace after Leonardo's death

### Francesco & Camilla:

- Distance due to his travels and her choice of convent life
- He never understood why she chose religious life
- Minimal visits, never really talked
- Her death teaches him about "too late"
- Parallel to Leonardo: both people he loved but didn't know well enough

### Lisa & the Portrait:

- Strange experience of being intensely seen yet invisible
- Leonardo saw something she didn't know she contained
- The painting captures her but also captures Francesco and Leonardo's relationship
- She's immortalized but as something other than who she was
- Final irony: famous forever for a smile she wasn't aware of making
